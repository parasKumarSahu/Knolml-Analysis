b'Carles & Sofia Piano Duo is the name of the duo of pianists consisting of Carles (Carlos) Lama, born 26 February 1970 in Girona, Catalonia, Spain, and Sofia Cabruja, born 11 May 1965 also in Girona.\nCarles and Sofia began playing together in 1987 and since then have performed four-hands piano recitals and two pianos and orchestra concertos in Europe, America and Asia.\n\nEarly life\nCarles Lama and Sofia Cabruja began their studies in their home town, Girona, and obtained the Superior Degree in Barcelona, at the Conservatori Superior de M\xc3\xbasica del Liceu.\nIn 1992, they were awarded a scholarship to pursue studies abroad and they enrolled at the \xc3\x89cole Normale de Musique de Paris, first, and later at The Hartt School (University of Hartford, Connecticut).\nWhile at university in the US, they both were awarded scholarships. The two professors who, according to them, has had the greatest influence on the duo are the Russian pianist Nina Svetlanova and the Brazilian pianist Luiz de Moura Castro.\n\nPerforming activity\nCarles and Sofia\'s first public appearance as a duo was in 1987; since then, they have developed an international career performing recitals in many countries of Europe, America and Asia.\n\nSome of their appearances include: Carnegie Hall in New York City, in February 2011, where they performed a program including Maurice Ravel\'s La Valse; Jin Mao Tower in Shanghai, in March 2007, in a four-hands recital including Spanish works by Alb\xc3\xa9niz and de Falla, or Teatro Sol\xc3\xads, Montevideo, Uruguay, where they were invited to present an Ibero-American program with works by Guastavino, Aceves and Basomba.Carles and Sofia have performed concertos for two pianos and orchestra or one piano-four hands and orchestra with several worldwide orchestras:\nIn 2004, they performed the concerto for two pianos BWV 1060 by Johann Sebastian Bach with the National Symphony Orchestra of Malaysia, at the Yayasan Seni Berdaftar, in Kuala Lumpur.In 2007, they premiered Thomas McIntosh\'s Concerto for piano four hands with the London City Chamber Orchestra, and in September 2011, they performed Poulenc\'s Concerto for Two Pianos and Orchestra in D minor with the State Hermitage Orchestra, at the Tzar\'s Village Music Festival.Carles and Sofia have taken part in special events and projects: In 2012, they were invited to participate in the Bach Marathon, held at the International House of Music, in Moscow, where they played Bach\'s concerto for 3 pianos and orchestra in D major, BWV 1063, and the concerto for 4 pianos and orchestra in A minor, BWV 1065.In 2013, they were asked to participate in a series of concerts of Beethoven\'s symphonies in chamber arrangements at the Fundaci\xc3\xb3n Juan March in Madrid, performing the 3rd and the 4th symphonies, arranged for piano four hands.\n\nRepertoire\nCarles and Sofia\'s repertoire ranges from Bach to 21st century, including all major works for piano four hands and orchestral transcriptions, with a special taste for the repertoire of the French, Russian and German composers.\nIn most of their concerts they perform Spanish music including works by Alb\xc3\xa9niz and de Falla.\nCarles and Sofia have developed a close relationship with many contemporary composers, like John Carmichael and Daniel Basomba, who after listening to the duo, created and dedicated works especially for them.\n\nWorld They give master classes and lectures in different European, American and Asian countries.\nSelect master classes and lectures include:\nIn 2001, in Tokyo, at the Steinway Hall, they gave a lecture on Spanish music.\nIn 2005, in Kuala Lumpur, they presented a workshop on Music & Literature, related to the acts of commemoration of the 400 anniversary of the publishing of Cervantes\' Don Quixote.In 2009, in Singapore, master class at Yong Siew Toh Conservatory of Music and at NAFA (Nanyang Academy of Fine Arts), in October.\nIn April 2013, master class at the University of Texas at Arlington.\nIn June 2013, in Italy, Carles gave a master class on the topic "The technique at the service of the art".\n\nArtistic directors\nCarles and Sofia have founded and act as artistic directors in several festivals and series of concerts.\n\nFestival de M\xc3\xbasica de Sant Pere de Rodes, taking place in Sant Pere de Rodes (El Port de la Selva) from 2001 to the present.\nFestival de M\xc3\xbasica de Besal\xc3\xba: taking place in the monastery of Sant Pere, in Besal\xc3\xba, from 2002 to 2012.\nHammerklavier Series: a cycle of piano recitals in Barcelona (from 2004 to 2009) and in Girona (from 2003 to 2010).\nLiederkreis Series: a cycle of lied recitals in Girona, with translation of all poems to Catalan (from 2003 to 2010).\n\nAwards and distinctions\nIn 2001, in Madrid, after a series of benefit concerts, her Royal Highness Margarita de Borb\xc3\xb3n, presented to Carles and Sofia an honorary distinction [1] [2] from UNICEF, in recognition of their humanitarian efforts.\nIn 2012, coinciding with their 25th anniversary, they have been recognized as Steinway Artists. This distinction was earlier granted to pianists and legends such as Arthur Rubinstein, Sergei Rachmaninoff or Alicia de Larrocha.\n\nPhilanthropy and mentoring\nIn 2010, Carles and Sofia founded Concerts4Good: Music on a Mission, a project seeking to promote solidarity among mankind, through the emotional power of music.\nConcerts4Good are international series of solidarity concerts which take place in different cities and countries. The first series of Concerts4Good took place in January 2013 in Porto, Portugal.\nCarles and Sofia also mentor some of the young, talented musicians and guide them along the path of music.\n\nReferences\nExternal links\nCarles & Sofia Piano Duo official website\nCarles & Sofia Piano Duo official blog\nCarles & Sofia Piano Duo official Youtube channel\nCarles & Sofia Piano Duo official Facebook page\nCarles & Sofia Piano Duo official Twitter page'
